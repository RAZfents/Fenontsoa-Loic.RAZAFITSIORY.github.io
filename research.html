Measuring Altruism: Precision vs. Simplicity (With Sabine Erica Kröger and Arnaud Dellis)

ABSTRACT: This paper proposes a simple measure of altruism for surveys. The measure is based on a revealed-preference analysis Varian (1982) using hypo- thetical dictator game decisions under various budget constraints, similar to Andreoni and Miller (2002). Using different approaches, we evaluate the measures (1) informational content and (2) precision in measuring altruism.

To analyze (1), we evaluate the statistical power of the experimental design to de- tect violations of the generalized axiom of revealed preferences and its gravity. Using the simulation and geometrical approach, in the spirit of Bronars (1987); Aizcorbe (1991), we propose a closed-form solution for a two-dimensional problem with three budget constraints. Furthermore, we consider the trade-off between high power and meaningful GARP violations, i.e. a low cost efficiency index Afriat (1972)

To assess (2), we evaluate the statistical power of the experimental design to identify the parameters of the utility function and compare it to Andreoni and Miller (2002) and standard Dictator Games.
