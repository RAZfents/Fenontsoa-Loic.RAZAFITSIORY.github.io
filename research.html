Measuring Altruism: Precision vs. Simplicity (With Sabine Erica Kröger and Arnaud Dellis)

ABSTRACT:

This project compares three approaches to measuring altruism: the Preference Values Survey (PVS), which relies on simple survey questions and a basic dictator game; the Andreoni and Miller (AM) method, which uses multiple dictator games with varying budgets to identify different altruistic motives; and the Dellis and Kröger (DK) protocol, a simplified design with three games and varying transaction costs. While PVS is easy to implement but limited, and AM is precise but complex, DK aims to balance simplicity and multidimensionality. We first assess their statistical power using the Bronars test and develop an improved version, DKR. A crossover experiment then evaluates whether DKR provides a reliable and scalable measure of heterogeneous altruism.
